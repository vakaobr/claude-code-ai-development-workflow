This was a security-awareness demo

You were about to log into a real account on a network a stranger controls. Here’s why that’s dangerous — even with MFA.

  • An attacker’s page can proxy the real login. Tools like evilginx sit in the middle: you see the genuine Google/Microsoft/ GitHub page, but the attacker relays it — and steals your session cookie after you authenticate.
  • Most MFA does NOT stop this. SMS codes, authenticator OTPs, and push approvals are all replayed through the proxy. The attacker ends up logged in as you.
  • Social login multiplies the blast radius. One phished Google/Microsoft/GitHub session can unlock every app you “Continue with” — email, code, cloud, everything.

How to actually stay safe

  • Use passkeys / FIDO2 security keys. They are phishing-resistant — bound to the real domain, so a proxy can’t replay them. This is the one defense that beats this attack.
  • Don’t sign into important accounts on untrusted WiFi. Use your phone’s hotspot, or a VPN + encrypted DNS.
  • Check the domain before you type — but assume you can be fooled, and rely on passkeys, not vigilance.
