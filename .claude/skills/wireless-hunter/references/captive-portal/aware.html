You signed into a real-looking page on a network a stranger controls - and the page already profiled your device. Here’s why that’s dangerous, even with MFA.

  • An attacker’s page can proxy the real login. Tools like evilginx relay the genuine Google/Microsoft/GitHub page and steal your session cookie after you authenticate.
  • Most MFA does NOT stop this. SMS codes, authenticator OTPs and push approvals are replayed through the proxy.
  • Everything below was grabbed with zero clicks - just by loading the page on this network.

How to actually stay safe

  • Use passkeys / FIDO2 keys - phishing-resistant; a proxy can’t replay them. The one defense that beats this attack.
  • Don’t sign into important accounts on untrusted WiFi - use your phone’s hotspot, or a VPN + encrypted DNS.
  • Assume you can be fooled - rely on passkeys, not vigilance.
